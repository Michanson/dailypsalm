Today's Reading

Psalm 46

God Is Our Fortress

1 God is our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.
2 Therefore we will not fear though the earth gives way, though the mountains be moved into the heart of the sea,
3 though its waters roar and foam, though the mountains tremble at its swelling.
Selah
4 There is a river whose streams make glad the city of God, the holy habitation of the Most High.
5 God is in the midst of her; she shall not be moved; God will help her when morning dawns.
6 The nations rage, the kingdoms totter; he utters his voice, the earth melts.
7 The Lord of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress.
Selah
8 Come, behold the works of the Lord, how he has brought desolations on the earth.
9 He makes wars cease to the end of the earth; he breaks the bow and shatters the spear; he burns the chariots with fire.
10 "Be still, and know that I am God. I will be exalted among the nations, I will be exalted in the earth!"
11 The Lord of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our fortress.
Selah

A thought for today

Finding stillness in the noise

In a world that rarely pauses, this psalm offers an invitation — not a command to do nothing, but a call to release the grip of anxiety and trust. The Hebrew word for "still" here, raphah, suggests letting go, relaxing the hands.

Whatever you're carrying today, there is room to set it down, even briefly, and remember who holds it all.

Sit with this Where in your day today might you find a single quiet minute — not to fix anything, just to be still?
